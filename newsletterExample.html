Upcoming Programs
 

3/3/2018 @ 10:30 am
Silent Sentinels
Dr. Cynthia Newton will present a program on women in history who exercised political power before they legally had any political rights. She will discuss historical examples, such as the Silent Sentinels who protested outside the White House for women’s suffrage, with specific focus on the contribution of Delaware’s women to that cause, and how they impact discussions of women and power today.
 
4/7/2018 @ 10:30 am

The Life of Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.
In remembrance of the 50th anniversary of his assassination in 1968, the Delaware Public Archives will present a program titled “The Life of Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.” Presented by Rev. Dr. John Moore, Sr. this program will focus on Dr. King’s life from his youth in Georgia, his years as the leader of the civil rights movement, the acceptance of the Nobel Peace Prize, and his tragic assassination in Memphis, Tennessee. Rev. Dr. Moore will also re-enact three of Dr. King’s most famous speeches: “We Shall Overcome,” “I Have a Dream,” and his final speech, “The Mountain Top,” presented the night before his assassination.
 
Upcoming Historical Marker Dedications
 
 
The Delaware Public Archives operates a historical markers program as part of its mandate. Markers are placed at historically significant locations and sites across the state.
